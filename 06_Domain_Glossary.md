# Ad Tech & Campaign Management — Glossary

## A

**Ad Exchange** — A digital marketplace where publishers sell ad inventory and buyers bid on it in real time via RTB.

**Ad Network** — A company that aggregates publisher inventory and sells it to advertisers. Older model, largely replaced by SSPs/exchanges.

**Ad Server** — Technology that stores, delivers, and tracks ad creatives. The authoritative source of impression/click data.

**Ad Tag** — A snippet of code placed on a publisher's page that calls an ad server to request an ad.

**Ad Unit** — A specific space on a publisher's webpage designated for advertising.

**Agency Trading Desk (ATD)** — A centralized programmatic buying unit within an agency or holding company.

**Attribution** — The process of assigning credit for a conversion to one or more marketing touchpoints.

**Audience Extension** — Publisher selling ads targeted to their own audience segments on third-party sites.

## B

**Banner Ad** — A rectangular graphical display advertisement embedded in a webpage.

**Behavioral Targeting** — Targeting audiences based on their online browsing or purchase history.

**Brand Lift Study** — Survey-based measurement of advertising's impact on awareness, perception, and purchase intent.

**Brand Safety** — Ensuring ads don't appear alongside content that could damage the advertiser's brand.

**Bumper Ad** — A short, non-skippable video ad (typically 6 seconds).

## C

**Campaign** — A coordinated set of ad activities with a single objective, budget, and time window.

**CDP (Customer Data Platform)** — A system that creates persistent, unified customer profiles from first-party data.

**Click-Through Rate (CTR)** — The ratio of clicks to impressions. CTR = Clicks / Impressions × 100.

**CMP (Creative Management Platform)** — Software for creating, managing, approving, and distributing ad creatives.

**Contextual Targeting** — Serving ads based on the content of the page, not the user's identity.

**Conversion** — A desired action taken by a user (purchase, sign-up, download, etc.) after seeing an ad.

**Conversion Rate (CVR)** — Conversions / Clicks × 100.

**Cookie** — A small file stored in a browser used to track user behavior across the web. Third-party cookies are being deprecated.

**CPA (Cost per Acquisition)** — Ad spend divided by number of conversions.

**CPC (Cost per Click)** — Amount paid each time a user clicks an ad.

**CPL (Cost per Lead)** — Ad spend divided by number of leads generated.

**CPM (Cost per Mille)** — Cost per 1,000 impressions. Most common pricing model in display.

**CPV (Cost per View)** — Amount paid for each video view.

**Creative** — The actual ad asset — image, video, HTML5 animation, copy, etc.

**CTV (Connected TV)** — Television content delivered via internet connection (smart TVs, Roku, Fire TV).

## D

**DCO (Dynamic Creative Optimization)** — Technology that automatically assembles personalized ad variations from modular components in real time.

**Deal ID** — A unique identifier for a programmatic deal between a buyer and seller (used in PMP, PG).

**DMP (Data Management Platform)** — Platform for collecting, organizing, and activating third-party audience data for targeting.

**DSP (Demand-Side Platform)** — Software that allows advertisers to buy ad impressions programmatically across multiple exchanges.

**DV360 (Display & Video 360)** — Google's enterprise DSP for buying display, video, and CTV inventory.

## E

**eCPM (Effective CPM)** — Revenue per 1,000 impressions from the publisher's perspective. = (Total Revenue / Total Impressions) × 1000.

**Engagement Rate** — Interactions (likes, shares, comments) divided by impressions or reach.

## F

**First-Party Data** — Data collected directly by the brand from its own customers (website, app, CRM).

**First-Price Auction** — Auction format where winner pays their exact bid.

**Flighting** — The period during which an ad campaign is active (start and end dates).

**Floodlight** — Google Campaign Manager's conversion tracking tag.

**Frequency** — Average number of times a unique user sees an ad within a given period.

**Frequency Cap** — A limit on how many times a user can see a specific ad.

## G

**GARM (Global Alliance for Responsible Media)** — Industry body that defines brand safety floor and suitability categories.

**Geo-Fencing** — Delivering ads to users within a defined geographic radius in real time.

**GRP (Gross Rating Point)** — Traditional TV/radio metric. Reach × Frequency. 100 GRPs = reaching 100% of target audience once.

## H

**Header Bidding** — Programmatic technique where publishers offer inventory to multiple ad exchanges simultaneously before the publisher's ad server makes a decision, resulting in higher yield.

**HTML5 Ad** — Interactive or animated ad built with HTML, CSS, and JavaScript (replaced Flash).

## I

**IAB (Interactive Advertising Bureau)** — Industry trade group that sets digital advertising standards.

**Identity Resolution** — Matching user data across devices and channels to create a unified profile.

**Impression** — A single instance of an ad being displayed.

**Insertion Order (IO)** — A formal document (contract) between advertiser and publisher detailing ad placement, price, and dates.

**Inventory** — Available ad space on publisher properties.

**IVT (Invalid Traffic)** — Non-human traffic such as bots that generate fraudulent ad impressions or clicks.

## K

**KPI (Key Performance Indicator)** — A metric used to evaluate campaign success.

## L

**Landing Page** — The webpage a user is directed to after clicking an ad.

**Latency** — The time delay in ad loading and serving.

**Line Item** — A unit of buying in an ad server or DSP representing a specific ad placement, with its own targeting, budget, and creative assignment.

**Lookalike Audience** — Audience segment that shares characteristics with an existing customer or prospect list.

## M

**Media Plan** — Document outlining which ad placements, channels, budgets, and timelines will be used in a campaign.

**MRC (Media Rating Council)** — Sets standards for audience measurement and ad viewability.

## N

**Native Advertising** — Paid content designed to match the look and feel of the surrounding editorial content.

**Negative Keywords** — In search advertising, keywords that prevent ads from showing for irrelevant queries.

## O

**OOH (Out-of-Home)** — Advertising encountered outside the home: billboards, transit, digital screens in public spaces.

**OpenRTB** — Industry-standard protocol for real-time bidding communications between buyers and sellers.

**OTT (Over-the-Top)** — Streaming content delivered directly via internet, bypassing traditional cable (Netflix, Hulu, etc.).

## P

**Pacing** — How quickly an ad campaign's budget is spent relative to its total schedule.

**Pixel** — A 1×1 transparent image or JavaScript tag used to track user actions.

**Placement** — A specific ad slot on a publisher's property.

**PMP (Private Marketplace)** — Programmatic deal where a publisher invites select buyers to bid on their inventory.

**Programmatic Guaranteed (PG)** — Programmatic deal with guaranteed impression volume at a fixed CPM.

**Programmatic Advertising** — Automated, data-driven buying and selling of digital advertising.

## Q

**QA (Quality Assurance)** — The process of reviewing ad creatives and campaign setup for errors before going live.

## R

**Reach** — The number of unique users who have seen an ad at least once.

**Remarketing / Retargeting** — Showing ads to users who have previously interacted with the brand's website or app.

**ROAS (Return on Ad Spend)** — Revenue generated per dollar of ad spend. ROAS = Revenue / Ad Spend.

**RTB (Real-Time Bidding)** — Auction-based buying of individual ad impressions in real time (~100ms).

## S

**Second-Price Auction** — The winning bidder pays $0.01 more than the second-highest bid (largely deprecated in favor of first-price).

**Segment** — A defined group of users sharing common characteristics.

**SKAdNetwork** — Apple's privacy-preserving attribution framework for iOS app campaigns.

**SSP (Supply-Side Platform)** — Technology that helps publishers manage, sell, and optimize their ad inventory.

## T

**Tag Management System (TMS)** — A tool (e.g., Google Tag Manager) that manages JavaScript tags on a website without code deploys.

**Targeting** — Defining which audience should see an ad based on demographics, behavior, context, etc.

**Trafficking** — The process of uploading, configuring, and distributing ad creatives to ad servers and platforms.

**Trading Desk** — A team or platform that manages programmatic media buying on behalf of clients.

## U

**Universal Pixel / Base Tag** — A single tag placed site-wide to enable audience building and conversion tracking.

## V

**VCR (Video Completion Rate)** — % of video ads watched to completion. = Completions / Starts × 100.

**Verification** — Third-party service (IAS, DV) confirming viewability, brand safety, and traffic quality.

**Viewability** — Whether an ad had the opportunity to be seen. MRC standard: 50% of pixels in view for 1 second (display) or 2 seconds (video).

**VPAID** — Video Player Ad Interface Definition — legacy video ad format (deprecated, replaced by OMID/VAST).

**VAST** — Video Ad Serving Template — IAB standard protocol for delivering video ads.

## W

**Walled Garden** — Closed advertising ecosystem where the platform controls data and targeting (Google, Meta, Amazon).

**Waterfall** — Legacy publisher ad selling method where each demand source is tried sequentially. Replaced by header bidding.

## X

**X-Device** (Cross-Device) — Targeting or measurement connecting user activity across multiple devices.
